Portuguese EuroAtlantic will receive Boeing 787 retired by AeroMexico

The aircraft in question is one of two 787-8 Dreamliners ordered by the Portuguese company from Aercap, a company that leases aircraft, that is, it buys the planes and leases them to the airlines..

The best answer, the right strategy

From the beginning, rumors arose that the aircraft would not be new, since EuroAtlantic has no tradition of taking new planes from the factory, in addition to the fact that the agreement was concluded with a leasing company and not with Boeing. This has now been confirmed. AeroMexico's N961AM registration jet, just seven years old, was returned to the lessor due to reduced demand caused by the coronavirus, according to the Mexican website Transponder1200. The 787 was delivered to the Mexican company in 2013 and has now been shipped to Arizona where it will be prepared for delivery to EuroAtlantic, which is due to receive the jet in European summer (mid-June and July). The transfer was confirmed by the PlaneSpotters website, although it does not mention the Portuguese registration number or the internal configuration..
