Timor Leste

Timor-Leste, officially the Democratic Republic of Timor-Leste (in Tetum: Timor Lorosa'e, officially Republika Demokrátika Timór-Leste), is one of the youngest countries in the world, and occupies the eastern part of the island of Timor, in Southeast Asia , in addition to the Oe-Cusse Ambeno exclave, on the north coast of the western part of Timor, the island of Ataúro, in the north, and the islet of Jaco, off the eastern tip of the island. The country's only land borders connect it to Indonesia, west of the main portion of the territory, and east, south and west of Oe-Cusse Ambeno, but it also has a maritime border with Australia, in the Timor Sea, the south. With 14 874 square kilometers of territorial extension, Timor-Leste has an area equivalent to the areas of the Portuguese districts of Beja and Faro combined, which is still considerably smaller than the smallest of the Brazilian states, Sergipe. Its capital is Dili, located on the north coast.
